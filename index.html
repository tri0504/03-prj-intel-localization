Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation. This shift sets the stage for decades of technological innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor. It ignites the microprocessor revolution and propels the future of computing devices.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture. That architecture drives countless PCs and servers in the modern era.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture. It ushers in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marks Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations. Intel invests in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and energy-efficient manufacturing to reverse the trend.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy and 2030 goals. The plan drives progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across its global operations by 2040. This builds on years of environmental initiatives.

2023

Renewable Electricity

The company achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide. That shift helps lower carbon emissions and advances long-term sustainability goals.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting suppliers, government officials, and industry leaders. Together they collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.
